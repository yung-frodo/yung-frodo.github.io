Past Experiences

I grew up in a household where both of my parents are doctors. As such, I began college believing that I wanted to become a surgeon. Once I rediscovered my fear of blood (and organic chemistry), I decided to try biomedical engineering. Within BME, I realized quickly that my main interests lie in the robotic aspects of what I was doing — the programming and wiring of my projects, rather than the mechanical or biological parts — so I decided to switch to Electrical Engineering.

As an E.E., I've focused strongly in software engineering, though I have not yet had much of a chance to explore my interests in hardware and computer engineering beyond the classroom setting. Read about my professional experiences in reverse chronological order below.
